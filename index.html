AI Classification of Emotions

Made by Rishita Rout and Divya Soni

This AI tool uses a Mobilenet image classification algorithm to distinguish between two emotions(Happy or Sad). The model has been trained using 800 Happy Images and 800 Sad Images using Google's teachable machine and tested using 50 images for each category.

Try it here by clicking on start button below


Quick Overview of the Project

Project Description: to develop a computer system that can accurately identify and categorize objects within images based on machine learning algorithms and training data.

Our Project Requirements:

- successfully distinguish the emotion between happy or sad

- easy for humans to understand

- have a 70% or higher accuracy

Details:

- includes all genders and a variety of cultures

- differentiates between two emotions; happy and sad

- trained with 800 images each

- tested with 50 images for each individual category

Overall Goal:

When patients visit the doctor, it can be tricky for the doctor to understand how they're feeling. Sometimes, patients might not say exactly how they feel, and doctors have to guess. But guessing isn't always accurate. This web-based tool, powered by AI, can look at your face and figure out whether you're feeling happy or sad. That way, if a patient is hiding their saddness, AI can detect it. This helps the doctor know how to help you feel better, and it makes the doctor's office a friendlier and more caring place for everyone.
